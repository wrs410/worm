Worm grunting is a fairly simple and clever technique for bringing earthworms to the surface. It involves driving a wooden stake into the ground and rubbing it with a metal object, like a saw or a metal rod. The vibrations from this rubbing produce a low, rumbling sound that travels through the soil.

Worms interpret these vibrations as the movements of moles, which are predators for them. In response, they instinctively come to the surface to avoid what they think is a threat underground. This method is often used by people who collect worms for fishing bait, and itâ€™s a time-honored tradition in some areas, particularly in parts of Florida.

The science behind it is based on the worms' natural defense mechanisms, and while it might seem a bit unusual, it's an effective way to gather worms without digging or using chemicals.
